Motor — electrical & mechanical dynamics
The motor converts input voltage to rotor angular velocity. The armature circuit (R, L) drives current; current produces torque through K; torque spins the rotor against its own inertia J_m and bearing friction.

Gearbox — speed reduction & inertia reflection
The gearbox steps down motor speed by ratio N = 4/1.5 ≈ 2.667. All downstream inertia (drum + load) gets divided by N² when reflected back to the motor shaft. It also hosts two of the four system bearings.
Gearbox relationship
ω_drum = ω_motor / N   ·   N = 4 / 1.5 = 2.667
J_reflected = J_downstream / N²   ·   b_reflected = b_downstream / N²

What is inertia mismatch?
When a motor drives a load, the load's inertia appears at the motor shaft — scaled by the gear ratio. The ratio of that reflected load inertia to the motor's own rotor inertia is the inertia mismatch ratio.
Inertia mismatch ratio
J_reflected
J_motor
=
J_gear/N² + J_drum/N² + m·r²/N²
J_m
A ratio of 1:1 is ideal — the load feels exactly as heavy to the motor as the motor's own rotor. When the ratio is much greater than 1, the motor is trying to accelerate an effective mass far larger than it was designed for. This leads to:
High mismatch (ratio ≫ 1)
Sluggish response · Long settling time · Reduced control bandwidth · Risk of instability · Motor overheating from sustained high current
Well matched (ratio ≈ 1)
Fast acceleration · Short settling time · Full control bandwidth available · Efficient power transfer · Good dynamic accuracy
The gear ratio N is the primary design lever — increasing N reduces all downstream inertias by N². This is why gearboxes exist beyond just changing speed: they are inertia transformers. The performance plot below shows exactly how your choices shift the dominant time constant.

Drum & load — rotational to linear
The drum converts shaft rotation to cable movement: x = r·θ. The hanging load m reflects as J_load = m·r² at the drum shaft, then /N² further to the motor shaft. This is typically the largest single contributor to inertia mismatch.
Drum kinematic relationship
x(t) = r · θ_drum(t)  →  X(s) = (r/s) · Ω_drum(s)
J_load_drum = m·r²   ·   J_load_motor = m·r² / N²
